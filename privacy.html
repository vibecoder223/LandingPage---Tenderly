1. Introduction

Tenderly, Inc. ("Tenderly", "we", "us", or "our") provides an AI-powered proposal automation platform for enterprise bid teams. This policy explains what personal data we collect when you use our website and product, what we do with it, and the rights you have over it.

We act as a data controller for the personal information we collect about our website visitors and account holders. When customers use Tenderly to process their own proposals, we act as a data processor for the content they upload, under the terms of our customer agreement.

This policy is written for the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. If you are outside the United Kingdom, additional protections may apply under your local law.

2. What data we collect

Information you give us directly

  • Account details: name, work email address, company, password (stored hashed, never in plain text).
  • Demo and trial requests: name, work email, company, phone number (optional), notes about your RFP process, preferred meeting times.
  • Support and contact messages: anything you choose to send us.
  • Content you upload to your workspace: RFP documents, past proposals, policy files, and the answers your team generates inside Tenderly.

Information we collect automatically

  • Usage data: pages visited, features used, broad geolocation derived from IP address, timestamps.
  • Device data: browser type and version, operating system, screen size, user agent.
  • Cookies and similar storage: see section 7 below.

Information we receive from third parties

  • Authentication data when you sign in via Google, Microsoft, or a configured SSO provider.
  • Limited information from analytics providers (aggregated and pseudonymous).

3. How we use your data

We use the data we collect to:

  • Provide and operate the Tenderly service, including running the AI features that read and draft proposal content for you.
  • Authenticate you, secure your account, and prevent fraud or abuse.
  • Respond to your enquiries, schedule demos, and provide customer support.
  • Improve our product, including aggregated and anonymised analysis of feature usage.
  • Send you service updates, billing notices, and (where you have opted in) marketing communications.
  • Comply with our legal and regulatory obligations.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not use your uploaded proposal content to train shared AI models.

4. Legal basis for processing

Under Article 6 of the UK GDPR, we rely on the following lawful bases:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): processing necessary to provide the Tenderly service you have signed up for, including account management and billing.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): running our business, improving our product, securing our systems, and contacting prospective customers about Tenderly when we believe it is reasonable to do so and your rights do not override our interests.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): marketing emails you have opted in to, optional analytics cookies, and any other processing where we ask you to opt in. You can withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): processing required to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and court orders.

5. Data retention

We keep personal data only for as long as we need it for the purposes set out in this policy, or as required by law.

  • Account data is retained for the lifetime of your account, plus up to 12 months after closure for backup and audit purposes.
  • Uploaded workspace content is retained while your account is active and deleted within 30 days of cancellation, unless you ask us to delete it sooner.
  • Demo and contact form submissions are kept for up to 24 months for sales and support follow-up.
  • Aggregated analytics data is kept indefinitely once it can no longer be associated with an individual.
  • Billing and tax records are kept for 7 years to satisfy UK accounting requirements.

6. Your rights

Chapter 3 of the UK GDPR gives you the following rights over your personal data:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: ask us to delete your data (the "right to be forgotten"), subject to legal exemptions.
  • Restriction: ask us to limit how we process your data.
  • Portability: receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Objection: object to processing based on legitimate interests, including direct marketing.
  • Withdraw consent: where we rely on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.
  • Automated decisions: ask for human review of any decision based solely on automated processing that significantly affects you.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at privacy@tenderly.ai. We will respond within one month.

If you believe we have not handled your data correctly, you also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

7. Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for three purposes:

  • Strictly necessary: session cookies that keep you signed in and remember your preferences. These cannot be switched off.
  • Analytics: first-party analytics that help us understand how the product is used. We set these only with your consent.
  • Functional: remember UI preferences such as dark mode or column visibility.

We do not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. You can manage cookies at any time through our cookie banner or your browser settings.

8. Third-party services

We rely on a small number of trusted providers to deliver Tenderly. Each is bound by a data processing agreement and processes your data only on our instructions.

  • Hosting and infrastructure: AWS (eu-west-2, London region by default for UK customers).
  • Email: Postmark for transactional email, Resend for marketing.
  • Analytics: a privacy-respecting first-party analytics provider, with IP addresses truncated.
  • Customer support: a help desk platform for managing support tickets.
  • Payments: Stripe, which stores card details on its own PCI-compliant systems (we never see your full card number).
  • AI inference: a contracted large-language-model provider, used under enterprise terms that prohibit training on your content.

A current list of subprocessors is available on request.

9. Data security

We take security seriously and use a combination of technical and organisational measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256).
  • Role-based access controls, least-privilege defaults, and audit logging.
  • Single sign-on and SAML support for enterprise customers.
  • Regular vulnerability scanning, penetration testing, and security training for staff.
  • SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 compliance (programme in progress).

No system is perfectly secure. If we ever experience a personal data breach that is likely to result in risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and you without undue delay, as required by Article 33 and 34 of the UK GDPR.

10. International transfers

Tenderly is headquartered in the United Kingdom and primarily processes UK customer data in the UK and the European Economic Area. Some subprocessors are based outside the UK or EEA.

When we transfer personal data outside the UK, we rely on one of the safeguards permitted by the UK GDPR:

  • Adequacy decisions issued by the UK government (currently covering the EEA, the UK Extension to the EU-US Data Privacy Framework, and certain other countries).
  • The International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses.
  • Other appropriate safeguards under Article 46.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify you by email or through the product before they take effect. The "last updated" date at the top of this page always reflects the current version.
